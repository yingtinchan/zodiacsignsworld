In Greek mythology, Virgo is the incarnation of the goddess of the earth, Demeter, and in some cases, the incarnation of her daughter Persephone. The evil god Astaroth, who is in charge of production, may refer to her as recorded in the Bible.

She is the splendid goddess of spring. She will open wherever she lightly steps. Full of delicate flowers. One day she and her companions were picking flowers on a meadow in the valley. Suddenly, she saw a silver daffodil with a sweet taste floating in the air. Persephone thought: "It is better than any of my flowers. Beautiful!” She was so beautiful, so she sneaked closer away from her companion, reaching out her hand about to touch the flower. Suddenly, a hole broke in the ground, and a carriage, driven by two black horses, rushed out of the ground. It turned out to be Pluto.
