Pisces (2.20 ~ 3.20)

Background

In Greek mythology, Pisces represents the incarnations of Aphrodite and Eros in the water. Aphrodite turned into a fish and hid in the Nile River (in other words, the Euphrates River) in order to escape the attack of Tiphon, the son of the earth goddess Gaia. Later, she discovered that she had forgotten to take her son Eros and escaped, so she went ashore to find Eros. To prevent separation from her son, she tied the two feet together, and then the two turned into fish shapes and dived into the river. Afterwards, Zeus lifted the fish that Aphrodite first incarnate into the air to become the fish constellation, and the two fishes tied together in the avatar of Aphrodite and Eros were called Pisces.
